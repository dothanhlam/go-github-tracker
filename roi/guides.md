# 📖 AI ROI Suite: Tab-by-Tab Explanation

## General Note on Data
⚠️ Important: The charts in this dashboard currently use Mock Data (Estimated Data) based on industry standards for tools like Cursor and Claude. To make this 100% accurate for our company, we will need to connect this tool to our GitHub API and Jira accounts in the next phase.

## Tab 1: Productivity & Growth
Goal: Show the "Big Picture" of how much extra work the team can do.

What this tab shows:
FTE Equivalent (Full-Time Equivalent): This is the most important number. It shows that our team of 6 is doing the work of 7.5 people.

Total Annual Savings: The dollar value of the time we "buy back" every year.

Capacity Chart: A line chart showing that as we use more AI, our "Pull Requests Completed" (Output) goes up, and our "Cycle Time" (Speed) goes down.

Why it matters:
This proves we don't need to hire more people right now. We are gaining 1.5 virtual developers for only $1,200 per month.

## Tab 2: Investigation & Intelligence
Goal: Show how AI helps us solve hard problems and fix bugs faster.

What this tab shows:
MTTR (Mean Time to Repair): How long it takes to fix a bug. AI reduces this from ~110 minutes to ~35 minutes.

The Discovery Gap: A comparison bar chart. It shows that "finding the bug" (Investigation) used to take 4 hours, but now takes less than 2 hours.

Risk Mitigation: Faster bug fixes mean our system is more stable and our customers are happier.

Why it matters:
Engineering isn't just "typing." It is "thinking." This tab proves that AI helps our team understand complex code much faster than before.

## Tab 3: Developer Breakdown
Goal: Explain exactly "how" the 25% gain is created.

What this tab shows:
The 50/30/20 Split: A stacked bar chart for all 6 developers.

50% (Coding): Writing the basic structure of features.

30% (Testing/QA): Automatically writing tests to ensure quality.

20% (Research/Docs): Finding answers in documentation instantly.

Individual Impact: Shows that every single developer—even those who are still learning—is creating more value than the $200 tool cost.

Why it matters:
It proves that AI helps with Quality (Testing) and Knowledge (Research), not just "Speed."

## Tab 4: Benchmark Comparison
Goal: Compare our team against a "Standard Team" that does not use AI.

What this tab shows:
The "Race" Chart: A side-by-side comparison of our team versus the market average.

Cost per Feature: Even though we spend $200 on the tool, our Total Cost per Feature is lower because we finish work so much faster.

Efficiency Gap: Shows that a standard team is "falling behind" because they still do everything manually.

Why it matters:
This makes the Director feel like we have a Competitive Advantage. We are delivering value at a lower cost and higher speed than other companies.
